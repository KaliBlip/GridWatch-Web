Frequently asked questions

I can't sign in — what should I check first?

Confirm you're using the email your organization registered with GridWatch. If your organization uses SSO, choose "Sign in with organization SSO" on the login screen instead of entering a password. If the problem continues, your administrator may need to re-invite your account.

A node shows "Offline" but I know it's powered — what does that mean?

Offline means GridWatch hasn't received a new reading from that node's sensor within the expected interval — it doesn't necessarily mean the transformer itself has lost power. Check the sensor's own connectivity (signal, wiring, or gateway) at the site. If several nearby nodes go offline together, the issue is more likely with the shared gateway or backend connection than with any single sensor.

Why did I get an alert but the current reading looks normal?

Alerts trigger from the reading at the moment a threshold was crossed. Tap the alert to open that node's detail screen and view the trend chart — you'll see the spike or drop, even if the metric has since returned to normal.

How do I change what triggers a push notification?

Go to Settings → Notification Preferences to choose which alert types (overload, overheating, low voltage, offline) send a push notification to your device.

I'm an administrator — where do I manage the ThingSpeak connection?

Go to Settings → Connected Channels. From there you can update the ThingSpeak channel ID, rotate API keys, and map channel fields (field1–field8) to the metrics shown in the app. This section is only visible to accounts with administrator permissions.

Can I use GridWatch without a data connection in the field?

The app needs a connection to fetch live and historical data. If you open a node you've viewed recently, GridWatch shows the last synced reading with a clear "last updated" timestamp so you know how current it is.

Report a problem

Include the node ID (if relevant), what you expected to see, and what happened instead — a screenshot helps. Send this to support@gridwatch.app and we'll follow up directly.
